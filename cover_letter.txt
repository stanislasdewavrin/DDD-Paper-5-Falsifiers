==========================================================================
SUMMARY
==========================================================================

I collect the falsifiable predictions of the DDD gravitational sector: bounds on Yukawa-type fifth forces from the cascade-throttled mobility, feedback effects in binary systems, and Eot-Wash-style equivalence-principle tests. Each prediction is quantified with the relevant experimental sensitivity. The aim is to give the community explicit handles on which the framework can be tested or ruled out.

==========================================================================
WHY THIS MATTERS
==========================================================================

A theoretical framework gains scientific status only when it makes falsifiable predictions. This paper consolidates those for the gravitational sector and identifies the most discriminating experiments.

==========================================================================
NOVELTY
==========================================================================

Quantitative Yukawa bounds from the DDD mobility. Predictions for binary feedback. Eot-Wash sensitivity analysis. Identification of the most discriminating near-future experiments.

==========================================================================
SUBMISSION STRATEGY
==========================================================================

This manuscript is Paper V of a planned ten-paper series on
Discrete Drainage Dynamics (DDD). The series is organised so each
paper has a clean, falsifiable claim of its own; the present paper
can be read independently of the others, with explicit cross-
references to its companions where needed.

I am happy to follow whichever route the editor prefers: standalone
submission of the present paper, or coordinated submission as part
of the series.
